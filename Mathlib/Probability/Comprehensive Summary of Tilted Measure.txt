  4. Key Insights for Cramér Lower Bound

  The standard proof strategy for the lower bound is:

  1. Change of measure: For fixed a > E[X], choose t^* such that deriv (cgf X μ) t^* = a
  2. Use tilted measure: Under μ.tilted (t^* * X ·), we have E[X] = a by integral_tilted_mul_self
  3. Apply Chebyshev/concentration: Use that under the tilted measure, X concentrates near a
  4. Relate probabilities: Use the density formula to relate probabilities under original and tilted measures
  5. Optimize: The rate function emerges from the optimization over t

  Most Useful Lemmas:
  - integral_tilted_mul_self - Connects tilted expectation to CGF derivative
  - tilted_tilted - Composition property
  - integral_exp_tilted - MGF under tilted measure
  - tilted_apply_eq_ofReal_integral_cgf - Explicit formula for tilted measure of sets
  - integrable_tilted_iff - Integrability characterization

  The tilted measure essentially "re-centers" the distribution around the point a we care about, making the lower bound proof more tractable!
